## Background & Motivation

I grew up in Marin County, just north of San Francisco—an area shaped by strong environmental values and a regional focus on sustainability. When the SMART train launched in 2017, it fundamentally changed how I viewed transportation. It made it possible for me to attend school 30 miles away in Santa Rosa and showed me how high-quality transit can expand access, connect communities, and strengthen a region.
  
That experience sparked my interest in sustainable mobility, rail systems, and the engineering decisions behind how cities function. I care deeply about improving transportation in the Bay Area, especially in the North Bay, where transit access is limited. Exploring San Francisco, Oakland, and Berkeley has further shaped my interest in how infrastructure, planning, and engineering influence the daily lives of the people they serve.


## Education & Experience
I graduated Cum Laude from Cornell University with a Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering and am currently completing a Master of Engineering in Systems Engineering through Cornell’s integrated undergraduate–graduate program, with an expected graduation date of December 2026.

As a mechanical engineering student, I focused on design, analysis, and prototyping. My projects have included rover wheel development, LoRaWAN device design, wind-turbine blade optimization, internal-combustion engine analysis, and structural component development for sustainable infrastructure systems.

Through my systems engineering studies, I have developed experience in Verification and Validation procedures, network optimization, decision-making under uncertainty, and human-centered engineering. These experiences have strengthened my ability to bridge detailed technical design with broader system-level considerations, allowing me to evaluate engineering problems through both mechanical, organizational, and community-oriented lenses.

## Engineering Interests
My engineering interests sit at the intersection of mechanical design, systems engineering, and transportation. I enjoy taking ideas from concept to physical reality. I’m also drawn to the systems side of engineering: understanding how subsystems interact, evaluating reliability, defining requirements, and using structured methods to design solutions that scale. Much of my work involves computational tools, including Python for simulation, numerical modeling, optimization, and data visualization. These technical skills connect directly to my passion for transportation: I’m especially interested in rail systems, multimodal mobility, and how engineering and planning come together to improve regional connectivity and urban development in urban settings. Ultimately, I aim to apply engineering tools and systems thinking to build more efficient, accessible, and sustainable transportation networks.

## Beyond Engineering
Outside of academics, I’ve been a member of the Cornell Men’s Alpine Ski Team. The sport has been part of my life since childhood, from skiing in Tahoe to visiting my dad in Sun Valley, Idaho and Oregon, where skiing is woven into the local culture. I also joined the Cornell Gymnastics Club, which connects back to practicing with my mom when I was younger.

I love to travel and explore new places. I’ve driven across the United States several times on different routes and visited multiple countries abroad. Experiencing different communities, cities, and ways of life has broadened my perspective and continually influences how I think about mobility, design, and infrastructure.
